## What is this?

Lead capture is the system that **records every inbound enquiry and routes it
into one place** so it can be followed up. For a service business, a "lead" is
anyone who raises their hand — a phone call, a website form, a WhatsApp message,
a Checkatrade enquiry, or a referral from a past customer.

Lead capture is not lead *generation* (creating demand) and not *sales*
(closing it). It is the connective tissue in between: the moment of first
contact, reliably caught and logged.

## Why does it matter?

Most service businesses lose more revenue to **leaks** than to a lack of leads.
A missed call at 4pm, a web form that lands in a spam folder, a quote request
written on a scrap of paper in a van — each is paid-for demand that quietly
disappears.

If marketing spend produces 100 enquiries and 30 are never logged or followed
up, the business has effectively burned a third of its marketing budget before
the sales process even begins. Fixing capture is usually the cheapest growth
lever a service business has.

## When should it be used?

Always, and from day one. Specifically, you need a defined lead-capture system
when:

- Enquiries arrive through **more than one channel** (phone + web + referrals).
- More than one person could answer the phone or read the inbox.
- The owner can no longer hold every enquiry in their head.
- You are spending money on marketing and want to measure what it returns.

## Step-by-step process

1. **List every channel** a lead can arrive through (phone, website form, email,
   social DMs, marketplaces, walk-ins, referrals).
2. **Define a single destination** — one pipeline or inbox every lead lands in,
   regardless of channel.
3. **Capture a minimum data set** for each lead: name, contact, job type,
   address/area, urgency, and source.
4. **Acknowledge immediately** — an automatic reply or callback promise so the
   lead knows they were heard.
5. **Assign an owner** so a named person is responsible for next contact.
6. **Set a follow-up deadline** (e.g. respond within one business hour).
7. **Record the source** so you can later tie revenue back to marketing spend.

## Common mistakes

- **Multiple inboxes.** Leads scattered across a personal phone, a shared inbox,
  and three marketplace apps with no single pipeline.
- **No acknowledgement.** The lead hears nothing for hours and books a
  competitor who picked up.
- **Not recording source.** Without it you can never tell which marketing
  actually works.
- **Capture depends on one person.** When they are on a job or on holiday,
  enquiries pile up unseen.

## KPIs

| KPI | What it tells you |
| --- | --- |
| Lead capture rate | % of enquiries that get logged vs. total received |
| Speed to first response | Time from enquiry to first human contact |
| Missed-call rate | % of inbound calls unanswered and not returned |
| Source attribution rate | % of leads with a recorded source |

## Software recommendations

- **CRM / pipeline:** HubSpot (free tier), Pipedrive, or a field-service tool
  like ServiceM8 or Tradify.
- **Call handling:** a VoIP system (Aircall, RingCentral) or a call-answering
  service for overflow.
- **Web forms:** the website form wired directly into the CRM, not just email.
- **Shared inbox:** a team inbox (e.g. Google Workspace shared mailbox) if a CRM
  is not yet in place.

## AI opportunities

- An **AI receptionist** that answers overflow calls, captures the minimum data
  set, and books or escalates.
- **Auto-summarisation** of voicemails and long enquiry messages into a clean
  pipeline record.
- **Smart routing** that reads the enquiry and tags job type and urgency
  automatically.

## Automation ideas

- Web form → CRM record created → instant SMS acknowledgement to the customer.
- Missed call → automatic text-back ("Sorry we missed you — reply here and we'll
  call straight back").
- New lead → task assigned to today's duty owner with a one-hour due time.
- Daily digest of any leads still untouched after the response deadline.

## Checklist

- [ ] Every lead channel is listed and feeds one pipeline
- [ ] Minimum data set defined and captured for each lead
- [ ] Automatic acknowledgement is sent within minutes
- [ ] Each lead has a named owner and a response deadline
- [ ] Lead source is recorded for every enquiry
- [ ] Capture works when the usual person is unavailable

## SOP template

> **SOP: Capturing an inbound lead**
>
> 1. When an enquiry arrives, create a record in [pipeline] within 5 minutes.
> 2. Capture: name, phone, email, job type, address/area, urgency, source.
> 3. Send acknowledgement [template] confirming we'll respond within 1 hour.
> 4. Assign the lead to today's duty owner.
> 5. Set follow-up due in 1 business hour.
> 6. If details are missing, contact the customer to complete the record.

# Stochastic Multi-Agent Consensus Report

**Problem**: Write a short (2-3 sentence) description for "Business Systems Library"
**Agents**: 8 (sonnet, general-purpose)
**Date**: 2026-07-02

## Consensus (agreed by 8/8 agents)

Three elements appeared as "essential" in every agent's output:

1. **Audience specificity** — name "service businesses with 5–50 employees." Makes the
   right reader self-select and filters out the wrong one. (8/8)
2. **Anti-blog / reference-library framing** — position it as a structured *reference*
   you look things up in and reuse, *not* a blog you scroll. Sharpest differentiator. (8/8)
3. **Concrete deliverables, named** — SOPs, workflows, checklists, KPIs, AI automation.
   Specifics over abstractions like "resources" or "operational excellence." (8/8)

## Divergences (genuine judgment calls)

- **Format vs. outcome opener** (~5/8 lead with format/reference; ~3/8 lead with the
  owner's pain/benefit). Feature-first reads authoritative; benefit-first converts.
- **Name AI automation explicitly?** 6/8 include it; 2/8 said it clutters a 2-3 sentence
  frame and belongs in a subhead/tagline.
- **Emotional close** — "a business that runs on systems, not on you" converts well but
  is more pitch than reference-desk tone.

## Outliers (1-2 agents — creative, worth stealing from)

- **Contrast hook**: "documents what business books skip" (contrarian).
- **Concrete topic examples**: dispatching, job costing, technician onboarding,
  scheduling, invoicing — makes the abstract word "system" tangible.
- **The plumbing wedge**: only the authority agent worked in "beginning with plumbing,
  expanding across the service trades" — good for an About page, too much for a hero line.

## Recommended synthesis

> **Business Systems Library is a structured reference for how service businesses actually
> run — the SOPs, workflows, checklists, KPIs, and AI automation opportunities that keep a
> 5–50 person team out of constant firefighting. Every system follows the same format: what
> it is, why it matters, the steps to implement it, and how to measure it. It's built to
> look up and reuse, not to scroll — a reference library, not a blog.**

### Shorter variant (meta description / ~150 chars)
